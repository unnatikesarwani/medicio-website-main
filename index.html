    Frequently Asked Questions

    What services do you provide?

    We provide a wide range of therapy services including occupational therapy (OT services), physical therapy, speech therapy, psychotherapy, and rehabilitation therapy. We also offer specialized programs like pediatric occupational therapy, cognitive rehabilitation, and sensory integration therapy for children and adults.

    What is occupational therapy?

    Occupational therapy helps people of all ages improve their ability to perform daily activities and live independently. Our occupational therapists use techniques such as physical rehabilitation, cognitive rehabilitation, and sensory integration therapy to support clients with autism, stroke recovery, ADHD, workplace injuries, and chronic pain management.

    Do you offer pediatric occupational therapy?

    Yes. Our pediatric occupational therapy program supports children with autism, ADHD, developmental delays, and sensory processing difficulties. We use engaging activities and play-based occupational therapy techniques to improve focus, coordination, and daily life skills.

    Do you provide therapy for workplace or sports injuries?

    Absolutely. Our occupational therapy for workplace injuries and physical therapy for sports injuries help individuals regain strength, mobility, and confidence to return to daily routines or athletic performance.

    What should I expect in my first session?

    During your first visit, your therapist (whether for occupational therapy, physical therapy, speech therapy, or psychotherapy) will assess your needs, discuss goals, and create a personalized plan using the most effective therapy techniques for your condition.

    How long is each therapy session?

    Most sessions last between 45–60 minutes, depending on whether you’re attending occupational therapy for stroke rehabilitation, psychotherapy for mental health conditions, or cognitive rehabilitation for traumatic brain injury.
